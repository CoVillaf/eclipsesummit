About the Conference

Eclipse technology is ubiquitous in the enterprise. At the same time, Eclipse continues to spur innovation and provides a platform for research in cutting-edge technologies.

Eclipse Summit India is presented by the local Eclipse Community in collaboration with The Eclipse Foundation. It is designed by the community, for the community. This is a great opportunity for the Eclipse fraternity in India to learn, explore, share, and collaborate on the latest ideas and information about Eclipse ecosystem and its community of projects with the core contributors, adopters, extenders, service providers, consumers and business and research organizations.

You will hear about successful industry deployments as well as highly innovative solutions in areas where you don’t expect Eclipse to be used. You will meet the experts and talk to them, network with your peers and share your experiences. You will also learn about the latest initiatives and industry working groups within the Eclipse ecosystem. You can talk to the industry experts about the Eclipse long-term support initiative, hear about Eclipse technology in safety-relevant environments (aerospace, automotive, public transport), learn more about the future of Machine-to-Machine technology, or see what we envision as the future build and management infrastructure for Eclipse projects.

Eclipse Summit India delivers networking opportunities that lead to synergies in the community, as well as opportunities to give and receive help on specific technical issues or to generate business opportunities.

Want to Present @ Eclipse Summit India?

To present at Eclipse Summit India 2016, please submit your proposal before June 30th 2016 via our public proposal submission system.

Unlike many other conferences, the sessions and keynotes at Eclipse Summit India are chosen by an independent program committee, allowing us to consistently offer a high-quality program. To contact the program committee, send us an email.

The themes for talks at Eclipse Summit India include the most interesting current topics. The 2016 conference program will include both hands-on tutorials and technical sessions on current topics pertinent to the Eclipse developer community, such as Eclipse Platform/RCP/Runtimes, Languages and Tools, Web/Mobile/Cloud Development, Modeling, Methodology and Devops, Embedded, IoT, Science, Java 9, IDEs and more, as well as sessions that demonstrate Eclipse-based solutions for finance, automotive, aerospace, insurance, and other domains. The exact themes are listed on the CFP site.
